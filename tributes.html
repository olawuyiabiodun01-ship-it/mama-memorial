Funeral Tributes

Words of Love & Remembrance

These tributes were delivered at the funeral of Mrs. Victoria Ayodele Arinke Olawuyi on the occasion of her home-going celebration. Each word is a testament to a life that touched all who came near it.

Her Children

Tribute to a Mother in Millions

Olusegun Olawuyi — Son

I give glory to God Almighty who in His infinite mercy brought me and my siblings into this mother earth through a honourable vessel in the person of Madam Victoria Ayodele Olawuyi — a.k.a. Eye Segun Alaso.

I consider it a great privilege and an uncommon honour to be reared, nurtured and tutored by you during your earthly ministry. You were the epitome of the virtuous woman as contained in Proverbs 31:10–31.

You spent most of your 72 years on earth in the service of God and humanity — a great disciple of our Lord Jesus Christ. Your commitment and devotion to kingdom assignment, and the way you dotted over all your children, sons and daughters-in-law including your adopted children, is a great challenge to me.

I am humbled by the zeal and zest with which you served your family (both nuclear and extended), the body of Christ, the Otun community and the entire human race.

You were a detribalised Nigerian — ever ready to accommodate and ever ready to entertain strangers. Most of my friends today are the people you brought into our home when they were stranded in Otun motor park on their transit from the northern part of the country to the south. You related cordially with everyone and you were a blessing to all. A landlady to Igbos, Hausas and even Ghanaians.

One evergreen lesson you taught us is that we must not be the last user of any material possession — be it clothes, shoes, etc. You said we should always give out cheerfully and the materials must be in good condition.

Whenever you came visiting to Ado Ekiti you would request for materials to be donated to the needy — that was how close the poor and needy were to your heart. Sometimes I wonder how you got the resources to cater for them. I know it is the Lord's doing and it is marvellous in my sight.

Nobody can replace you in the heart of all and sundry. A lover of peace to a fault! You said if someone was on his way to attend a church service and he met some people quarrelling, that person should stop and settle the rift before proceeding to church — that that singular action is an acceptable service to God as well.

I cannot forget the day you obtained the registration form of Ifesowapo Cooperative Society for me. You insisted that I must have "Ajeseku" — I must put some money aside for the rainy day. That was how I got the money to buy my first car. You would always quote your uncle and mentor, Baba Freeman John Olatunde: "If you have money, the best thing to do is either to save it or commit it to a project — otherwise it will grow wings and fly away because you will spend it on frivolities."

When it was time in your sight for me to build a house, you invited me and said: "Segun, a kii ko ola lo si imogun ode o" — you said I should go and acquire a minimum of two plots and start my building project, and you supported me both morally and financially.

One thing is sure — going to Otun will never be the same again. Never! Who will buy Iru (locust beans), Ogbokuru and other stuffs for me and my wife again? Melo ni a fe ka leyin adepele! No wonder, when the news of your demise reached our people, the whole town stood still and I was told the market was hurriedly closed.

For all these and other testimonies, I give glory to God Almighty for your life — a life well spent. I'm gonna miss you.

Sun re o, Mama Segun. Mama Funmilola, Mama Bukola, Mama Funto, Mama Bola and Mama Abiodun. Adieu, ma mi.

Life does not consist in duration but in donation. Mummy, you were able to impact so many lives positively — chief among them is Segun Olawuyi.

Mum, you were an achiever. A very prosperous woman in your own way — even though you did not acquire abundant wealth, your treasure is well laid in heaven and a crown of glory is awaiting you.

Your demise is a sermon of sorts. So many people have promised to reconsider their ways and emulate you, so that when it is their turn to leave this mother earth — which is inevitable anyway — glorious testaments will be said concerning them.

On behalf of myself and my siblings, I make bold to say: we cannot pray for a better mother. Good night and rest in perfect peace in the bosom of our Lord Jesus Christ, until the resurrection day when we shall meet to part no more. We love you, but the will of God must be done.

— Olusegun Olawuyi

Tribute to a Great Mother. My Teacher and Lover.

Funmilola — Daughter

You are the next in my life, after the Holy Spirit, my sweet mother. You were my teacher in words and in deeds. From pregnancy till you closed your eyes in death, you kept teaching me. I learnt the acts of kindness, love and care I try to manifest from you. You also taught me trading, home keeping, child training and even how to socialise and combine work with rest. No dull moment with you. You related well with all that come in contact with you without discrimination. Sought the good of all people irrespective of tribe or status. Loved and saw to the welfare of not just your family but every member of your extended family. You easily forgive and forget.

I thank God for making me come to this world through you. I did not prepare for this sudden departure but has to submit to the will of our creator. Therefore I say, "Now unto the King eternal, immortal and invisible, the only wise God, be honour and glory for ever and ever, Amen" — I Timothy 1:17. I believe we will meet to part no more on the resurrection morning.

— Funmilola

My Priceless Mother

Oluwafunmito (Funto) — Daughter

Rev. 14:13 — "Blessed are the dead who die in the Lord… That they may rest from their labors and their work follow them."

Mummy was too much for words to describe. My ever beautiful mother, my role model, my inspiration, my friend and my confidant — you were more than a mother to me, you were my world. You taught me how to be a lady, a wife and a mother. You are a mother of inestimable value. Your children were the centre of your world; nothing was too much to sacrifice for the well-being of your children. You denied yourself pleasures so we could be somebody in life. You never gave up on us even against all odds. You were so proud of us because you — with the help of God — made us who we are today.

You were our father and mother at the same time. We were ready to take the world because Mummy is always there. I never knew you would leave me so soon. I am writing you a tribute so soon, mother — we spoke about many things, we had so many great plans ahead. I check my phone, waiting to hear from you, but you have not called in the past 3 weeks. Life has not been the same without you. Ohhhh, I have missed you. Who can be compared to you? You were just too much, Mum. You were too loud to be shut down. You made your mark on anything you touched. I just wished you could have stayed a little longer…

You gave your all. You were a loving wife and mother, a loving mother to all, selfless and so transparent. You had no heart to keep a grudge — a friend of all, peacemaker, and fully committed to God. You were a true Christian in word and in deed. You instilled discipline into us all. You showed us the way to God and instilled moral into us. With a wink of your eye the message is passed and understood. A true wife to your husband even till death — an amiable and kind woman. I will spend the rest of my life reflecting on your attributes and building on the legacies of your sacrificial giving and love. Your impact was felt by all and sundry. Who will not be proud of a mother like you? If I have to come to this world again, I will come through you over and over again.

Ayodele Arinke… you came, you saw, and you conquer. I am proud to be called your daughter. I will always love you… rest on, Mum.

— Oluwafunmito

My Mother – My Friend

Bolanle Ololade Apeke — Daughter

Mummy — mummy! You were such an amazon, adorable, elegant and virtuous woman. You exhibited all the fruits of the spirit — love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness and temperance.

What a courageous, extraordinarily brave and fearless woman. You excelled among your equal and stood out among the crowd. Mother, you were not just my mother, you were my friend — not just my friend but my best friend! My inspiration, my confidant! No amount of eulogy and encomium can perfectly describe or qualify how unique and special you are to me. You were one of a kind! Truly, the world just lost an angel. You never gave up on me even when I was at the verge of death — you hoped against hope, put your faith in place, and the Lord honoured your faith and brought me back to life! You were a mother in Israel — my prophetess, my judge, my counsellor, my instructor. You were a caregiver and a philanthropist. The 'Deborah' and 'Dorcas' of your generation. You touched lives in divers ways; you were so many things to so many people.

The Lord had used you as an instrument to put smiles on many faces and to be a solution — a change agent to your world. Eye mi, Eye Bolanle Apeke! You were a blessing to me and to your generation.

"The most wonderful thing in the world is someday who knows who they are and knows where they are going and knows what they were created to do." — Bishop T.D. Jakes

Mom, yours was a purpose-driven life. You knew your ministries and walked worthy of your calling — Reconciliation, meditation, charity and selfless service to man and humanity. Your legacies in these areas live on.

Eye mi, you lived a practical Christian life — taking Jesus with you wherever you went. You were so proud to be a vessel unto honour in God's hand and a channel through which God's blessings flowed to others. You exemplified the Proverbs 31 woman — hardworking, industrious, loving and a homemaker. My mother, you were a point of reference for good!

You were such a loving, caring wife, companion and soul mate to my father — your husband. You loved my dad "in poor, in rich… in sickness and in health" till death parted you. You stood by my father and kept loving him till he took his last breath — what a virtuous woman. Your price is far above rubies. In life… in death, you are priceless. You were a mother in million, the best of them all. A worthy example to emulate — you taught us to be loving, caring and submissive wives to our husbands. My role model, my mentor, my friend.

You gave us all you had — love, time, care, listening ears, empathy and financial support. You sold your possessions and got loans to give us the best education. You believed in us and wanted nothing but the best for your children. Your push, encouragement and support has brought us this far because you would not allow us to settle for less! Eye mi, Arinke O gboye, omo a fojo gbogbo dara bi okinni. Wow, you were simply the best of all mothers. I thank God for the privilege of using you as a vessel to conceive, bring forth and raise me in the fear of the Lord and to become who I am today. You filled my life with so much love! Unforgettable are the memories of time we shared together. You were always there for me; we laughed, danced, rejoiced, cried and prayed together. You left when I needed you most. I was caught unaware — even when you said goodbye, I thought it was one of your jokes. I instantly said this must be an expensive joke because I would not give up on you!

My heroine, you were a rally point, a unified factor among your families. You valued relationships and accommodated all. Your opinion on any issue within your family was always well accepted; you spoke as an authority. You never looked down on anyone — you loved and provided for all, whether they are related or not. Your hospitality and love for the less privileged is matchless considering your financial status! You taught us to appreciate anyone and be a blessing to anyone that comes our path in life. And that giving is not always about the quantity but the spirit behind such giving.

"There is no greater gift you can give or receive than to honour your calling. It is why you were born. And how you become most truly alive." — Oprah Winfrey

Mom, you were distinguished among your peers. You sowed love, time, and energy into so many lives. You were mother to the motherless, friend to the lonely and abandoned, comfort to the broken-hearted, strength to the feeble knees and a shoulder to lean on for others. You left indelible legacies in so many hearts, Mom! You lived your life to the fullest. You honoured your callings and fulfilled destiny.

My matriarch, you did not allow your background and little or no formal education to hinder you. You rose above every limitation — you practically taught yourself how to read and write, and you made yourself heard. You became voice for the voiceless, help to the helpless and hapless… Eye Segun, Akoni Obinrin! Obinrin bi Okunrin! I just love you, Mom, and I thank God who made your life this beautiful!

The titaness imparted her generation positively and they all testified that you came and made a difference. Your life is worthy of celebration — life well lived and spent! So proud of you, Mom! Indeed, it is not how long but how well. You left so soon, so sudden, Mummy… even if you had departed at the age of 120, it would still be so soon. No one will ever want to lose a mother like you. People like you do not die in the heart of those you touched. You live on in my heart because you are unforgettable and irreplaceable!

My Amazon, you were a paragon of inner beauty and virtue. When you took your last breath, Mom was just like a sleeping beauty! You came, you ruled your world and conquered. You fought a good fight, you had finished your course and kept the faith. Henceforth there is laid up for you a crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous judge shall give you — and not only to you but unto all them also that love His appearing (2 Timothy 4:7–8).

It is the end of the matter that matters. My mom stayed with the Lord till the very end. "Far be it from God that he should do wickedness; and far from the Almighty that he should commit iniquity…" (Job 34:10–12). You were not cut off, Mom — you were caught up. My consolation is from John 6:43–44: "No man/woman comes to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him/her and I will raise him/her up at the last day."

My matriarch, my heroine, my titaness, my best friend and my Amazon — you have been caught up in glory. No space can contain who you were and most importantly who you are to me. It is only God that can fill up the vacuum that your demise has created in our hearts!

Eye mi Arinke — sincerely speaking, it is so. LOVE YOU NOW AND ALWAYS. You are deeply and sorely missed, angel… sleep on, my mom, my friend!

— Bolanle Ololade Apeke

A Timeless Treasure

Olawuyi Adetunji Abiodun — Son

"The fragrance of flowers spreads only in the direction of the wind. But the goodness of a person spreads in all directions." — Chanakya

I am so proud to have known you. Mother, you were more than a mother to me — you were the truest and dearest. I called you dad and I called you friend. You stood alone for 18 years after our dad passed on, and only God knows how you got the inner strength to cope during this period.

You were precious, a gift from God. You touched my heart in so many ways. Your strength and smile even on dark days made me realise I have an angel beside me.

Your words of advice are still very fresh: "Remember that you are not like any other person; remember your past; and remember the home you come from." With these words, you would pray for me.

Mother, I knew you didn't want to leave us. You heard God's whisper, calling you home — you wanted to be with God but you didn't want to leave us yet. I saw you fighting it. You loved us so much that you held on tight, until all your strength was gone and you could no longer hold on. Finally you gave your hand to God and slipped away quietly without bidding us farewell.

You taught me to never give up on my dreams but to persevere no matter how hard the storm may be.

Precious mother, you have lived well, loved much. You were staunch, strict and sometimes very stubborn. And you were strong! Beautiful threads of your life are intricately woven into ours through the tapestry of God's limitless grace and love.

Thank you for helping us to spread our wings and fly. Thank you for helping us to grow and flourish. Thank you for helping to guide our steps as we have journeyed along the pathway to a deeper walk with God. You have had an amazing life journey. And today your sisters and brothers, your grandchildren, family and friends pay you great honour and respect.

Thank you, Mother, for all you have done for us — the words seem so small when the gratitude in my heart is so large! To God be the glory for gracing us with your love.

As our community rises up and calls you blessed — we your children are most blessed because you gave us life. We are richly blessed by your life; we have been blessed by your sacrifice and devotion, your thoughtfulness, your loyalty; we are blessed by the grace of your love. Now your life on earth has ended but we have peace and comfort, knowing in great faith that you are home in heaven with God and all things have become new. You are singing a new song with the celestial music of the Angelic Choir.

Rest now in the loving arms of Jesus, knowing that you are greatly loved. We will miss you; we cherish you. You are a precious gift to us from God. We are blessed by an indelible, rich heritage and our birthright of Christian faith. You are gone from this earth but far from gone from our lives — your love and memory will be an eternal flame to give our family strength and bond us together in love in greater ways than ever before.

And yes, Mother — "you are truly timeless!" Your beauty, grace and love transcend all time and space. Thank you for your unwavering love and giving heart. Thank you for taking the journey with us. Thank you for all the treasured memories over all these years.

Thank you, Mother — you are a timeless treasure! Heart of our hearts, you leave with us a strong bond of love that can never be broken — a lasting living legacy for your children and grandchildren, because you taught us that God's principles are timeless and they alone provide us with an unshakeable foundation in the saving grace of Christ Jesus our Lord and Saviour, upon which to raise our families a lasting eternal legacy to pass to the next generation, knowing that your love will go on and on forever, binding us together as God's love flows in rivers of living water throughout generations.

I love you in the whole wide world!

I will forever miss you!

— Olawuyi Adetunji Abiodun

Sons & Daughters-in-Law

A Tribute to Our Precious Mother

Prof. Oladipo (Baba Tosin) — Son-in-law

Mama was a fulfilled person, fulfilled in all her earthly assignments. I am sure she died very happy with herself. Mama also used her God-given profits from her small cloth selling business to touch the lives of relations and several ordinary people. She was a source of joy to all of us and those who came across her.

Our own sweet mother, Mama Victoria Ayodele Olawuyi, is in Heaven with Jesus, smiling down upon us today, as we join together to celebrate a life well spent. Honour is not about a perfect childhood. Tribute to Our Departed Mother, Mother-in-Law and Our Friend

Olumide Babatunde Oyeneye — Son-in-law

Words are not enough to describe the late Mrs Victoria Ayodele Arinke Olawuyi. Even though I only knew you for a very short period of time, it's like I had known you for ages. How on earth will I forget the first day I met you — having been told how strict, down to earth and "no nonsense" kind of a person you were, fortunately you received me with open arms and open heart! It was a shock for me to take in the fact that you were no more with us on this side of eternity. Nevertheless, I thank God for the loving-kindness and the joy I experienced from you.

The conversations I had with you were periods of joy, happiness and fun. I would have loved to share these experiences with you for a longer time but God knows best and loves you more. I believe you are at peace at the bosom of our Lord Jesus Christ where there is neither pain nor grief.

The moment I set my eyes on you I knew that I had found a mother in you. You prayed for us and with us till you died. I am one of the beneficiaries of the good upbringing you gave your children. They are well cultured, raised and God fearing. When I talked with you I could hear my wife's voice through you! And in the length of our various discussions, I knew where your daughters picked their smiling faces, laughter, sound mind and intellectual skills.

"Mama lawyer" as you fondly called yourself — the best and greatest gift you gave me was your baby girl, your friend, and I promised you I would take good care of her. We had so many plans for you, Mom, but God has the best plan for you because He has a good thought towards us.

You were always a subject of laughter and joy as we remembered your sense of humour and frank talks on issues. A real mother through and through! I pray that those of us you left behind will be able to build on your glorious accomplishments while at the same time praying for the repose of your dear soul.

I hereby end this tribute with Psalm 133:1 that I believe sums up your life through your goodness of heart and pleasantness in pooling people together and advancing their cause in life.

I know you are up there watching over us. We love you and believe we shall see each other at the feet of our Lord Jesus Christ. Bye for now but not forever — I love you.

— Olumide Babatunde Oyeneye

Her Grandchildren

A Force of a Woman

Oluwatosin, Bukola & Tobiloba Oladipo — Grandchildren

Barely five feet tall, she was a force of a woman — the sparkle in her eyes not dulled by age, a great woman. The 72 years she lived are one heck of a milestone. I am humbled and honoured to get to say a few words about my grandmother. She was an outgoing and fun-loving woman who enjoyed the company of her family and friends. Iya alasho as she is fondly called, lived life to the fullest. We have many fond memories of my granny — she taught me how to stand up and be a man, a few lessons on how to sell materials, taught my sister how to knit, and always shared her life experiences whether you are willing to listen or not, because she knew it will be beneficial to all.

I say this not in regret but with joy in my heart, knowing she always put God and family first and wanted the best for all of us. One of my aunts said she flew away like an angel that she is, and my consolation is in the fact that she was caught up and not cut off. There is no mincing words — she loved the Lord and served Him with all her heart, and yet He asked her to come home. She left in Christ; she is a Victor, like her name implies. Although we will miss her very dearly, let's celebrate her wonderful life and share her memories and make sure her print lives on.

Love you, Granny, and we will forever miss you. Knowing fully well how much she loved to dance, I can confidently say she danced her way to heaven. Love you, Granny, now and forever.

— Oluwatosin, Bukola & Tobiloba Oladipo
