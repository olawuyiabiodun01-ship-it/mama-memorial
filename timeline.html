Her Journey

A Life Well Lived

1942

Born into the Bankole Family

Born on 23rd May 1942 to Late Pa. Joseph Bankole (Baba pele-pele) of Odo-Oja Compound, Otun Ekiti — one of the first renowned commercial drivers of his time — and Mama Alice Adunola Bankole of Igboogun Compound, Otun Ekiti. Ayodele Arinke was her mother's firstborn.

Early 1940s

A Bright Mind Denied Schooling

She briefly attended Seventh-day Adventist Primary School, Eleti, where her brilliance so impressed her teachers that they pleaded for her to continue — but her father would not relent. The door of formal education was shut by the prevailing bias against girl-child education, though the light in her could not be extinguished.

Late 1940s

Life at Lokoja — Learning Languages, Milling Grain

Ayodele Arinke and her cousin, Late Folorunso Obebe, went to live with their Uncle Pa. John Olatunde (Baba Freeman), a grain miller in Lokoja. Working in his mill for years, she thrived in the cosmopolitan city and became fluent in both Hausa and Nupe. They later relocated to Akure to continue the business.

1957 – 1959

Apprenticeship in Cloth Trading

She returned to Otun Ekiti to help her mother raise her younger siblings, and during this period learnt the cloth-selling trade under her uncle, Late Pa. Ajayi Obaaro, and his wife. By the close of her apprenticeship she had mastered the business thoroughly and was ready to stand on her own.

1959 onwards

A Pacesetter in Textile Trade

She launched her cloth-selling business and became one of the most reputable textile merchants in Mobaland — a true pacesetter who brought glamour into the trade. Many of today's cloth sellers in the region passed through her tutelage. She was known for quality, integrity and an eye for beautiful fabric.

1961

Marriage to Pa. Mathew Olawuyi Osasona

Vibrant, pleasant and beautiful, "Ayus" — as friends called her — chose to marry from her hometown. Her marriage to Late Pa. Mathew Olawuyi Osasona was celebrated in 1961, a union shaped by deep family ties and abiding love. The marriage was blessed with five children. She was embraced wholly by her husband's family as their own flesh and blood.

1961 – 2014

A Proverbs 31 Wife and Home Builder

Through every season — abundance and hardship, health and prolonged illness — she stood as her husband's greatest champion. She kept covenant faithfully, nursed him through his illness without complaint, and he would often say: "Many daughters have done virtuously, but you excel them all." Together they gave their five children qualitative and sound education.

1990

Adult Literacy — Best Student in Ondo State

Never believing it was too late to learn, she enrolled in the Adult Literacy Education programme organised by the Ondo State Ministry of Education. She graduated as the best student in her class and was recognised as one of the overall best students across the entire state — a vindication of the brilliance that should have been nurtured decades earlier.

Community Service

Leader, Advocate, Treasurer, Builder

She served tirelessly across multiple community roles: executive member of Egbe Omo Otun Ekiti, member of Better Life for Rural Women and the Family Support Programme in Moba Local Government, Treasurer of the Parent Teachers' Association of Moba Grammar School for many years, and member of the Market Development Committee responsible for building shops and market infrastructure.

Lifelong Faith

Dorcas of Our Time — Adventist Women's Ministry

A devout Seventh-day Adventist, she was an ardent member of the Dorcas Society (now Adventist Women Ministry). Her personal ministry mirrored Dorcas in the Bible — she could not stop giving, loving, caring and encouraging until her last breath. Her charity and hospitality crossed every boundary of tribe, language, status and religion. She was a peacemaker, a health advocate, and a living sermon.

May 2014

Last Joyful Outing — Joke's Wedding

Her last social outing was on 29th and 30th May, 2014, when her daughter Joke got married. She celebrated with all the joy, music and dancing that defined her — none who were there knew it would be her final farewell to this world.

2014

Called Home to Glory

On 28th June 2014, she was caught up in glory. Like an angel — she flew away. Gone from our sight, never from our hearts. Her words remain with us: "Ranti omo eni ti iwo nse" — Remember the child of whom you are.

In loving memory of Olawuyi Victoria Ayodele Arinke

23 May 1942  ·  28 June 2014
